About Me

I am a Physics Engineering student with a strong focus on mathematical modeling, computational physics, and numerical simulation applied to real-world engineering and research challenges. My experience includes developing physics-based models using Lagrangian mechanics, differential geometry, and advanced numerical methods, as well as designing automation and analysis tools for industrial simulation workflows.

My work spans the computational modeling of mechanical systems, digital twins, and optical and geometric simulations, alongside data-driven approaches implemented in Python, Julia, Matlab, and scientific computing frameworks. I am particularly interested in the intersection of physics, optimization, and artificial intelligence, with applications in advanced simulation, photonics, and quantitative problem-solving across engineering and industry.
