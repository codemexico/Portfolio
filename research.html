Geodesic Curves on Curved Surfaces

Differential Geometry Research with Dr. Héctor Medel

A research-oriented numerical study centered on the modeling and simulation of geodesic paths on curved manifolds using computational tools. The work highlights the full research pipeline: conceptual formulation, mathematical modeling, numerical implementation, and visualization of results.

Approach

The project is complemented by an animation that communicates the system's behavior dynamically and intuitively within a specific manifold. The implementation leverages differential geometry principles and numerical integration methods.

Python Julia Differential Geometry

Physics-Informed Neural Networks

Scientific Machine Learning

Development of a Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) framework to solve ordinary differential equations by embedding physical laws directly into the training loss function. The approach leverages automatic differentiation to enforce governing equations and initial conditions without relying on labeled data.

Approach

Advanced architectural features such as Fourier feature encoding and residual connections are included to improve convergence. A graphical interface allows users to define equations and visualize solutions interactively, making the project both technically robust and user-friendly.

Quantum Wave Packet Simulation

Quantum Mechanics

Exploration of the time evolution of a quantum mechanical wave packet interacting with a potential barrier. Starting from analytical solutions of the Schrödinger equation, a Gaussian wave packet is constructed via superposition and evolved numerically in time.

Approach

The project investigates tunneling, reflection, and transmission phenomena, connecting quantum theory with visualization. An interactive executable application allows users to explore the dynamics of the wave packet in real time, demonstrating fundamental quantum mechanical principles.

Exoplanet Detection Using Neural Networks

Astrophysics & Machine Learning

Implementation of an automated pipeline for the classification of exoplanet transit signals from TESS light-curve data. Physical preprocessing techniques, including detrending and period detection, are combined with a one-dimensional convolutional neural network (CNN).

Approach

The model outputs probabilistic classifications distinguishing confirmed exoplanets from false positives. The project demonstrates the integration of astrophysical theory with modern machine learning, visual outputs, and a standalone executable application.
