Rotary Cutter Dynamic Model

John Deere

Complete dynamic modeling of a rotary cutter system using the Euler–Lagrange formalism with dissipative Rayleigh terms. The system is modeled as a set of coupled nonlinear differential equations describing the rotational motion of the disk and blades under realistic operating conditions. The project was part of a work collaboration with John Deere.

Approach

Numerical integration via fourth-order Runge-Kutta (RK4) to simulate the system's response, enabling analysis of torque requirements, blade dynamics, and energy dissipation. The model bridges theoretical mechanics with an applied industrial context.

Molecular Dynamics Simulation with Langevin Thermostat

Stochastic Thermal Systems

A molecular dynamics simulation framework for modeling interacting particles under thermal fluctuations. The dynamics are governed by Newton's equations of motion and integrated numerically using the Velocity–Verlet algorithm.

Approach

A Langevin thermostat was incorporated to introduce viscous damping and stochastic noise, reproducing canonical ensemble statistics and realistic temperature control. The simulation captures particle trajectories, velocity distributions, energy evolution, and diffusive behavior. Interparticle interactions are modeled using the Lennard–Jones potential, enabling the study of collective effects and equilibrium properties. The project establishes a clear link between microscopic particle dynamics and macroscopic thermal behavior.

Python Matplotlib LAMMPS

Data-Driven Stochastic Optimization of Inventory and Pricing Policies

Machine Learning and Metaheuristics

An integrated framework for the stochastic optimization of inventory management and pricing strategies in a real-world economic system. The methodology combines predictive modeling, Monte Carlo simulation, and hybrid optimization techniques to address uncertainty and variability in demand.

Approach

A neural network model forecasts demand based on historical data, capturing nonlinear relationships and temporal patterns. These predictions are embedded into a stochastic simulation of daily operations, evaluating system performance under fluctuating conditions. Optimization is performed using a genetic algorithm to explore the decision space, followed by simulated annealing to refine solutions and improve convergence. The framework provides a scalable and generalizable approach to multi-objective optimization, balancing profitability and operational stability.

Stochastic Optimization of Packing Fraction Using Simulated Annealing

Geometric Optimization

Development of a simulated-compression algorithm for maximizing the packing fraction of hard disks confined within a two-dimensional box. The algorithm determines dense, non-overlapping configurations by stochastically optimizing both particle positions and the effective size of the containing domain.

Approach

The algorithm proposes random displacements of particles, followed by an evaluation of an objective function that penalizes overlaps and inefficient spatial arrangements. Configuration updates are accepted or rejected according to a Metropolis-type criterion. A compression mechanism gradually reduces the confining box size, coupling configurational optimization with geometric compression. As the temperature decreases, the algorithm transitions from exploratory stochastic motion to deterministic refinement, converging toward configurations with high packing fraction.
